## Getting Started

Open the app in any modern browser on your phone, tablet, or computer. No account needed, no installation required — it runs entirely in your browser.

**Recommended device:** Phone or iPad held in portrait orientation. The app was designed for touch screens first.

At the top of the screen you'll see:

- **Guitar Progressions** — the app title
- **All Levels / Beginner toggle** — switches between showing everything or just beginner-friendly content
- **Four tabs** — Learn, Songs, Play Along, Tuner

Tap any tab to switch between sections.

## The Learn Tab

This is your home base for studying chord progressions.

### Step 1: Pick a Progression

You'll see a grid of progression cards like **I vi IV V** and **I V vi IV**. Each card shows:

- The progression in Roman numerals
- Which volume it comes from (Vol. 1, 2, or 3 — ordered from simplest to most complex)

**Tap a card** to select it. It will highlight with a colored border.

### Step 2: Pick a Key

After selecting a progression, a row of key buttons appears (A, A#/Bb, B, C, ... G#/Ab). 

**Tap a key** to see the actual chord names for that progression in that key. For example, "I vi IV V in G" becomes **G, Em, C, D**.

> **Beginner tip:** If Beginner Mode is on, some keys will be grayed out — those require barre chords. Keys with a green dot are beginner-friendly (all open chords).

### Step 3: Explore Positions

Once you pick a key, a **Position** row appears with buttons like "Open Position," "Fret 3," "Fret 6," etc. These show you the same chords played at different spots on the guitar neck.

- **Open Position / Pos 1** — uses open chord shapes near the nut (easiest)
- **Higher positions** — use barre chords and moveable shapes further up the neck

**Tap a position** to see the chord diagrams update.

### Reading Chord Diagrams

Each chord card shows:

- **Difficulty tag** — Green "Open," gold "Partial," or red "Barre"
- **Chord name** — e.g., "G" or "Am"
- **Roman numeral** — e.g., "I" or "vi"
- **Fretboard diagram** showing:
  - **Vertical lines** = strings (left is low E, right is high E)
  - **Horizontal lines** = frets
  - **Red dots** = where to place your fingers
  - **Gold dots** = the root note of the chord
  - **"O" above a string** = play it open (unfretted)
  - **"X" above a string** = don't play this string (mute it)
  - **Thick bar across strings** = barre (press one finger across multiple strings)
  - **Number on the left** = starting fret number (if not at the nut)
  - **Thick line at top** = the nut (fret 0) — means you're at the top of the neck

## The Songs Tab

A library of famous songs organized by which chord progression they use.

### Browsing Songs

Each song entry shows:

- **Song title and artist**
- **Progression** — which Roman numeral pattern it uses
- **Key** — what key the song is in
- **Learn button** — tap to jump directly to the chord shapes for that song

### Filtering

At the top, you'll see filter buttons for each progression (I vi IV V, I V vi IV, etc.). Tap one to show only songs that use that progression. Tap **"All"** to see everything.

### Learning a Song

When you tap **"Learn"** on a song, the app:

1. Switches to the Learn tab
2. Selects the song's progression
3. Selects the song's key
4. Shows you the chord diagrams

From there, you can explore different neck positions for those chords.

## The Play Along Tab

This is where you practice playing in real time with a scrolling chord display.

### Setting Up

1. **Progression** — choose from the dropdown (defaults to I vi IV V)
2. **Key** — pick your key from the dropdown
3. **BPM** — set the tempo with the slider or type a number directly (40–200 BPM)
4. **Metronome** — check/uncheck to enable click sounds on each beat
5. **Beats per chord** — how many beats before the next chord (2, 4, or 8)

### Starting

Tap the **Start** button. Several things happen:

- The setup controls collapse into a **compact bar** showing something like "I vi IV V in C · 80 BPM" with pause and stop buttons
- A **position picker** row appears (Open Position, Fret 3, Fret 6, etc., and Random)
- **Chord diagrams** appear showing three chords: the previous, current, and next
- The **scrolling highway** starts animating

### The Highway

The dark area at the bottom is the highway — a Guitar Hero-style scrolling display:

- **Colored blocks** scroll from right to left, each representing one chord
- **Gold vertical line** labeled "NOW" marks where you should be playing
- **Chord name** is displayed large inside each block
- **Roman numeral + number** appears below the chord name (e.g., "vi (6)")
- **Beat dots** at the bottom of each block fill in as beats pass
- **Stats bar** at the very bottom shows current chord, beat count (e.g., "3 / 4"), and loop number

### Position Picker

While playing, you can tap position buttons to change which neck position all chord diagrams show:

- **Open Position, Fret 3, Fret 6, etc.** — locks all chords to voicings near that fret
- **Random** (gold button) — randomizes the position for each chord change. This forces you to learn shapes all over the neck. The next-chord preview shows you what's coming so you can prepare.

### The 3-Chord Preview

Above the highway, three chord diagrams are displayed side by side:

- **Left (faded)** — the previous chord (what you just played)
- **Center (full size)** — the current chord (what you should be playing now)
- **Right (faded)** — the next chord (what's coming up — get ready!)

### Controls While Playing

- **Pause button** (two vertical bars) — freezes the highway and metronome. Tap again (play arrow) to resume.
- **Stop button** (square) — stops everything and returns to the setup screen.

### Suggested Starting Settings

| Level | BPM | Beats per Chord | Position |
|---|---|---|---|
| Just starting | 50–60 | 8 | Open Position |
| Getting comfortable | 70–80 | 4 | Open Position |
| Building speed | 90–120 | 4 | Fixed position |
| Challenge mode | 80–100 | 4 | Random |

## The Tuner Tab

Helps you get your guitar in standard tuning (E A D G B E).

### Reference Tones

Six buttons represent each string:

| Button | String | Note | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Thickest (lowest) | E2 | 82.41 Hz |
| 5 | | A2 | 110.00 Hz |
| 4 | | D3 | 146.83 Hz |
| 3 | | G3 | 196.00 Hz |
| 2 | | B3 | 246.94 Hz |
| 1 | Thinnest (highest) | E4 | 329.63 Hz |

**Tap a string button** to select it. The note name and frequency are displayed large in the center.

**Tap "Play Tone"** to hear the reference pitch through your speakers. The tone plays for about 3 seconds. Tune your string to match.

### Microphone Tuner

**Tap "Listen"** to activate the microphone tuner.

- Your browser will ask for microphone permission — tap **Allow**
- Play a string on your guitar
- The display shows the **detected note** and **frequency**
- The **gauge needle** swings left (flat) or right (sharp)
- The status message shows how many **cents** off you are

| Status | Meaning | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| Green "In tune" | Within 5 cents | You're good! |
| Red "X cents flat" | Pitch is too low | Tighten the tuning peg |
| Gold "X cents sharp" | Pitch is too high | Loosen the tuning peg |

Tap **"Stop"** to turn off the microphone.

> **Note:** The microphone tuner requires HTTPS or localhost. If you're accessing the app over plain HTTP, the Listen button will show an error message.

## Beginner Mode

Toggle the switch at the top of the page from "All Levels" to "Beginner."

When Beginner Mode is on:

- **Keys with barre chords are hidden** — only keys where every chord in the progression can be played with open shapes are available
- **Green dots** appear on beginner-friendly keys
- **Practice tips** appear with specific advice for the selected progression
- **Songs are filtered** to only show those in beginner-friendly keys
- **Position 1 (Open Position)** is emphasized

This is perfect for your first weeks of learning. Once you're comfortable with open chords, turn Beginner Mode off to unlock all keys and barre chord shapes.

## Understanding Roman Numerals

Roman numerals are how musicians talk about chord progressions without being tied to a specific key. Here's the cheat sheet:

| # | Numeral | Type | Feel | Example in G |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **I** | Major | Home base — where the song feels "resolved" | G |
| 2 | **ii** | minor | Stepping stone — gentle movement | Am |
| 3 | **iii** | minor | Moody — adds depth | Bm |
| 4 | **IV** | Major | The lift — bright energy | C |
| 5 | **V** | Major | Tension — wants to go back to I | D |
| 6 | **vi** | minor | The emotional one — bittersweet | Em |

**Uppercase = Major** (bright, happy sound). **Lowercase = minor** (darker, sadder sound).

The beauty is that the pattern stays the same in every key. "I V vi IV" always feels like "home, tension, sad, lift" whether you play it in C, G, or any other key. The chord names change, but the feeling doesn't.

The app shows Roman numerals alongside chord names everywhere — on the Learn tab diagrams, on the Play Along highway, and in the progression cards — so you'll naturally absorb this system as you practice.

## Tips for Practice

### For Complete Beginners

1. **Turn on Beginner Mode** — it filters everything to open chords only
2. **Start with I IV V** (the simplest 3-chord progression) in the **key of G** — that gives you G, C, D (all easy open chords)
3. **Study the diagrams** on the Learn tab until you can form each chord
4. **Go to Play Along**, set BPM to **50**, beats per chord to **8**, and just try to switch between chords in time
5. **Don't worry about strumming patterns** yet — just get the chord changes smooth

### Building Speed

1. Start at a comfortable BPM where you can make every chord change cleanly
2. Increase by **5 BPM** each practice session
3. When you can play a progression at 100+ BPM cleanly, try a **new key**

### Learning the Fretboard

1. Once you're comfortable with open chords, turn **Beginner Mode off**
2. Practice the same progression at **Position 2, 3, 4, and 5** on the Learn tab
3. In Play Along, use **Random mode** — it forces you to jump between positions and builds real fretboard knowledge

### Using the Song List

1. Find a song you love on the Songs tab
2. Tap "Learn" to see its chords
3. Practice those chords on the Learn tab
4. Switch to Play Along with the same progression and key
5. Pull up the actual song on your phone and play along with both!

### Practice Routine (20 minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0–3 min | Tune your guitar using the Tuner tab |
| 3–8 min | Study a new progression on the Learn tab, practice chord shapes |
| 8–18 min | Play Along at a comfortable BPM, gradually increase |
| 18–20 min | Try Random mode or a new key for a challenge |
